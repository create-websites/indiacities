Discover Punjab's Rich History: Museums & Landmarks


Museums and Historic Places to Visit in Punjab

Name Location Description
Lahore Museum Lahore The Lahore Museum is one of the oldest and largest museums in Pakistan. It has a collection of over 20,000 objects, including ancient coins, manuscripts, and paintings.
Badshahi Mosque Lahore The Badshahi Mosque is one of the largest mosques in the world and was built by the Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb in the 17th century. It is a beautiful example of Mughal architecture.
Rohtas Fort Jhelum The Rohtas Fort was built in the 16th century by the Afghan king Sher Shah Suri. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is known for its impressive architecture and strategic location.
Harappa Museum Sahiwal The Harappa Museum is located near the site of the ancient Indus Valley Civilization. It has a collection of artifacts from the civilization, including pottery, jewelry, and tools.
Golden Temple Amritsar The Golden Temple is a Sikh temple that is known for its beautiful architecture and golden dome. It is a place of worship for Sikhs and is open to visitors of all religions.
