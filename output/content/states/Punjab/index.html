Top 5 Must-Visit Places in Punjab


Place Description
Golden Temple The Golden Temple, also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib, is a Sikh gurdwara located in the city of Amritsar. It is one of the most revered spiritual sites in Sikhism and attracts millions of visitors every year. The temple is made of gold and is surrounded by a large pool of water, which is said to have healing properties.
Jallianwala Bagh Jallianwala Bagh is a public garden in Amritsar that is known for the Jallianwala Bagh massacre that took place there in 1919. British troops opened fire on a peaceful gathering of Indians, killing hundreds of people. The garden now serves as a memorial to those who lost their lives in the massacre.
Wagah Border The Wagah Border is a border crossing between India and Pakistan that is known for its daily flag-lowering ceremony. The ceremony is a display of military pageantry and is attended by thousands of people every day. It is a symbol of the long-standing rivalry between the two countries.
Anandpur Sahib Anandpur Sahib is a holy city in Punjab that is known for its gurdwaras and temples. It is the birthplace of the Khalsa, a Sikh warrior community that was founded by Guru Gobind Singh. The city is also known for its Hola Mohalla festival, which is celebrated every year in March.
Qila Mubarak Qila Mubarak is a historical fort located in the city of Bathinda. It was built by the Mughal emperor Akbar in the 16th century and has since been used as a residence by various rulers. The fort is now a popular tourist attraction and is known for its beautiful architecture and intricate carvings.
